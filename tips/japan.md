## Transport / Transporte

### IC Card (Suica / PASMO)
- Get at airport vending machines or JR counter at Narita
- Works on: trains, buses, convenience stores, vending machines, coin lockers
- Load with cash at any station machine
- *Funciona en trenes, buses, konbinis, máquinas expendedoras y lockers*

### JR Pass / Rail Pass
- **Evaluate if worth it** for our itinerary:
  - Tokyo → Hakone → Kyoto → Nara → KIX
  - 7-day JR Pass: ~¥50,000 per adult
  - May be cheaper to buy individual tickets / *Puede ser más barato comprar boletos individuales*
- Kids 6–11 pay half price on JR. **Lyam (4y) rides free** / *Lyam (4 años) viaja gratis*
- Infants ride free / *Bebés viajan gratis*

### Getting Around
- **Shinkansen (bullet train)**: Reserve seats, especially with kids / Reservar asientos
- **Metro/subway**: Excellent in Tokyo and Kyoto. Follow color-coded lines
- **Google Maps**: Works perfectly for transit directions in Japan
- **Taxis**: Clean and safe but expensive. Use for short distances with tired kids

> *El transporte público en Japón es increíblemente puntual. Los trenes salen al segundo exacto.*

## Money / Dinero

### Cash vs Card / Efectivo vs Tarjeta
- **Japan is still cash-heavy** in smaller shops and restaurants
- Major stores, hotels, and chains accept credit cards
- Withdraw yen at **7-Eleven ATMs** (accept international cards) / *Cajeros de 7-Eleven aceptan tarjetas internacionales*
- Budget: carry ¥10,000–20,000 in cash per day for family

### Tipping / Propinas
- **No tipping in Japan** — it can be considered rude / *No se da propina en Japón*

## Food / Comida

### Convenience Stores (Konbini)
- **7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart** — Amazing food quality!
- Onigiri (rice balls), sandwiches, bento boxes, fresh fruit
- Hot food: fried chicken (karaage), nikuman (meat buns)
- Great for quick kid-friendly meals / *Excelente para comidas rápidas para niños*

### Kid-Friendly Food / Comida para Niños
- **Ramen**: Most places have kid-size portions / Porciones para niños
- **Udon**: Thick noodles, easy for kids to eat / Fideos gruesos, fácil para niños
- **Curry rice (kare raisu)**: Japanese curry is mild and kids love it
- **Tamagoyaki**: Sweet rolled omelette / Omelette dulce enrollado
- Family restaurants: **Gusto, Saizeriya, Jonathan's** — Cheap, kid menus, drink bars

### Baby Food / Comida para Bebé (Diego, 1y)
- Baby food available at drugstores (マツモトキヨシ / Matsumoto Kiyoshi)
- Look for "離乳食" (rinyu-shoku) = baby food
- Konbini also have soft foods suitable for infants

## With Kids / Con Niños

### Stroller Tips / Consejos para Coche
- Most stations have elevators (look for エレベーター signs)
- Strollers allowed on trains (use wheelchair spaces)
- Many attractions have stroller parking / *Muchas atracciones tienen estacionamiento de coches*

### Changing Rooms / Cambiadores
- Available in most department stores, malls, and stations
- Look for 🚼 signs
- **Tokyo**: Excellent baby facilities everywhere

### Kid Activities They'll Love
- 🎮 Arcade machines and gachapon (capsule toys) — everywhere!
- 🚂 Watching Shinkansen at stations
- 🦌 Feeding deer in Nara
- 🎨 TeamLab Planets (immersive)
- 🛝 Ueno Park playground
- *¡Las máquinas de gachapon son adictivas! Llevar monedas de ¥100*

## Etiquette / Etiqueta

- 🚫 **No eating while walking** (except festival streets) / No comer caminando
- 🔇 **Quiet on trains** — set phones to silent / Silencio en trenes
- 🚮 **Few public trash cans** — carry a bag for trash / Llevar bolsa para basura
- 👟 **Remove shoes** at temples, some restaurants, ryokans / Quitarse zapatos
- 🙇 **Bow slightly** when greeting / Inclinarse ligeramente al saludar

## Weather in September / Clima en Septiembre

- **Tokyo**: 22–30°C (72–86°F). Humid. Possible typhoons
- **Kyoto**: Similar to Tokyo but slightly hotter
- **Pack**: Light clothing, rain gear, comfortable walking shoes
- *Septiembre aún es caluroso y húmedo. Llevar ropa ligera y paraguas.*
